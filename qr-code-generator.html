Frequently asked questions

What can I put in a QR code? +
You can encode any URL, plain text, email address, phone number, SMS message, Wi-Fi credentials, or vCard contact info. Most smartphones can scan all of these natively without any app.
Are these QR codes free to use commercially? +
Yes — completely free, no watermark, and usable for any personal or commercial purpose. No attribution required.
What size should I use for printing? +
For most print materials (menus, flyers, business cards), use at least 400px. For large-format signage, use 600px. A QR code should be at least 2cm × 2cm when printed.
What is error correction and which level should I choose? +
Error correction allows a QR code to be scanned even if part of it is damaged or obscured. Low is fine for clean digital uses. High is recommended if you plan to place a logo over part of the QR code or use it in environments where it might get dirty.
Do these QR codes expire? +
No. QR codes generated here are static — they encode the data directly. As long as the URL or content you encoded is still valid, the QR code works forever.
