Frequently asked questions

Why should I shorten a URL? +
Short URLs are easier to share on social media, in print, and in messages. They look cleaner, are less likely to break in emails, and on platforms with character limits (like Twitter/X), a short URL saves valuable space.
Do shortened URLs expire? +
It depends on the service. When connecting a real URL shortening API, link expiry depends on the provider — many free tiers keep links active for 30–90 days. Paid tiers usually offer permanent links.
Are short links safe to click? +
Short links created from legitimate services are safe. Always be cautious with short links from unknown senders. When in doubt, you can preview a link by adding a "+" to the end (e.g. toolhub.io/abc123+) with services that support previews.
Can I use short URLs on a QR code? +
Yes — and it's a great idea. Shorter URLs encode into simpler, less dense QR codes that scan faster and more reliably. Use our URL Shortener first, then paste the short link into the QR Code Generator.
